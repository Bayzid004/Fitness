4 Simple Stretches to Try at Home

Maintaining an active lifestyle is crucial for our overall health and well-being, particularly in preventing cancer. One way to counteract the effects of prolonged sitting and inactivity is through regular stretching. In this blog post, we will share some simple stretches from the Anticancer Lifestyle Program Course o help increase blood flow and keep the joints mobile, including the Quadriceps Stretch, Hip Flexor Stretch, Hamstring Stretch, and Calf Stretch. Incorporating these stretches into your daily routine can help promote physical and mental wellness, as well as reduce the risk of chronic disease.

How to Develop a Mindful Movement

You may have read the title of this post and wondered what could possibly be the connection between mindfulness and movement. The reason mindfulness is so important in movement is because as the mind begins to wander to that “to do list” or the “emails you have yet to answer”, your attention wanders away from your postural alignment and from your breath. For instance, think about the last time you were sitting and working on your laptop, or getting lost in your smartphone scrolling on social media. What did your posture look like? Probably not great. Slouching puts a strain on our frame and compromises our ability to breathe properly.
